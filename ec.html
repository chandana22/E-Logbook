R V College of Engineering



Department of Electronics and communication


The Electronics and Communications Department commenced in the year 1969 and now runs one undergraduate, two postgraduate programs (MTech in VLSI & Embedded Systems and M Tech in Communication Systems) and PhD programs under VTU. The programs offered by the department are the most sought after branch in Engineering by top CET (conducted by Karnataka examination authority) rank holders and other aspirants from across the country. All the three programs are accredited by the National Board of Accreditation (NBA), and are compulsorily outcome based, structured to meet the needs of Industry and Research. The program contents are upgraded on a biannual basis with involvement from industry and reputed research institutions. A total of 44 experienced faculty members all of whom are postgraduates and ten of them with PhDs as well,support three core specializations (VLSI, wireless & wireline Communications as well as Image & multimedia processing) in the Department. Academics at UG and PG level is sustained with a staff to student ratio of 1:13 and well supported by licensed version of basic software tools in over 200 computers. The curriculum includes a strong self-study component and mini projects at all levels so as to develop the studentsâ€™ independent analytical abilities. To support research across the specializations, an excellent infrastructure comprising of Software tools (Cadence etc.), Software Simulation environment (FEKO, HFSS EM flow solvers, VxWorks with specific Board support packages etc.) and state of the art equipment (Network Analysers, Channel simulators etc.) are available in the department. Apart from PhD oriented academic research in the core areas, the department has been involved in a wide range of application oriented research supported by grants received from DRDO, ISRO and Industry for current topics in underwater communication, Image processing and high performance Compact antennas.
